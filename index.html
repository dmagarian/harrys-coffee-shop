Our Story

A Brooklyn Dream on the California Coast

In 1960, Harry and Cathy Rudolph brought a piece of Brooklyn to the sunny shores of La Jolla. Inspired by the coffee shops and diners of Harry's native New York, they created a neighborhood gathering place with vinyl booths, a long counter, and the warm smell of fresh coffee.

Over six decades later, Harry's remains La Jolla's oldest restaurant ‚Äî now run by the next generation of Rudolphs. John Rudolph and Liz Rudolph-Gotfredson have carried forward their parents' legacy, expanding to a second location in Del Mar while keeping the spirit of that original Brooklyn diner alive.

The walls still feature Norman Rockwell prints and vintage sports memorabilia, and the regulars still come for the same reason they always have: honest food, great coffee, and the feeling of being home.
